## Current Manifesto (For Reference)

The existing manifesto, published on the homepage:

> "I work at the intersection of classical design and full-stack engineering. The systems I build serve creative teams and neurodivergent founders who need structural support—not another SaaS dependency, but infrastructure they can own and operate.
>
> This site indexes that work, organized by intent: the governing logic, the production deployments, and the creative practice."

**Analysis:** This manifesto emphasizes what is built (systems at the intersection of design and engineering) and who it serves (creative teams, neurodivergent founders), but it doesn't claim the actual positioning: how meaning survives deployment. It positions the work as outcomes (systems that serve) rather than as methodology (how thinking shapes systems). It also doesn't explain why this site looks different from typical portfolios—why extraction content exists, why thinking is visible.

---

## New Manifesto (Proposed)

I build narrative infrastructure—systems where story and technical architecture work as one. Not systems that tell stories. Systems where the narrative *is* the structure.

The constraint I solve: how does meaning survive deployment? How do you design so intent holds steady through technical implementation, visual expression, and operational reality? That requires thinking across domains—narrative, technical, visual—and making them coherent, not just compatible.

This site shows both the work and the thinking. Years of iteration, decision-making, failed experiments, and what survived. Not just deliverables. The methodology visible. Why this approach. Why those constraints shaped these solutions. What changed when theory met practice. This transparency is the proof: if the thinking holds up under scrutiny, you can trust the methodology to work for your project.
